Executive Summary 1
Introduction
The electric vehicle (EV) market has seen explosive growth, with a wide range of models—from affordable to luxury—becoming available to consumers. By 2023, the global EV market reached a significant milestone, capturing over 10% of car sales in certain regions and surpassing 10 million units sold globally.
In this interactive Power BI project, various DAX measures and KPIs are utilized to provide visual insights into the EV adoption trends in Washington (WA) state. The dashboard showcases how WA has successfully integrated different EV models and makes into its market as of 2024, offering a comprehensive view of the state's transition towards electric mobility.

Background
Electric vehicle adoption has surged in the last two decades. Early on, limited models and a nascent market hindered growth. However, the 2010s witnessed a significant shift. Government incentives, stricter emissions regulations, and increased environmental awareness spurred consumer interest. Automakers responded with a wider range of models, making EVs more accessible. By 2019, the global EV market had gained traction, though still a small percentage of overall car sales. The 2020s marked a pivotal period. Major automakers committed to electrifying their fleets, and the EV market expanded significantly.


Problem statement
Despite the growing interest in electric vehicles (BEVs), a significant portion of the population is still hesitant to make the transition. Many potential buyers are concerned about the limited driving range of EVs compared to traditional gasoline-powered cars, fearing they might run out of battery before reaching a charging station, especially on longer trips. While all-electric vehicles can typically travel between 110 and over 300 miles on a single charge, their plug-in hybrid electric vehicle (PHEV) counterparts usually offer only 15 to 60 miles on battery power alone, which contributes to range anxiety.
