Methodology

Data Preparation
The data cleaning process involved handling missing values, standardizing formats, removing duplicates, correcting inconsistencies. This preparation ensured that the data was structured, enriched, and optimized for predictive modeling and visualization.




Model Selection
Classification-based model was used to predict the likelihood of vehicle adoption based on characteristics such as location, vehicle make, model and year, Key columns like Vehicle ID, County, City, State,  could provide insights into regional trends, while Electric Range and Make/Model were important factors that influences consumer decisions and adoption. Columns like Electric Vehicle Type and Clean Alternative Fuel Vehicle (CAFV) Eligibility are particularly useful in determining if certain EVs meet government regulations or incentives, which can significantly affect adoption.



Model Development
In this phase, we identified the GradientBoostingClassifier as the best-fit model and trained it using electric vehicle data to analyze trends across various vehicle models manufactured in different years. The primary goal was to predict future adoption rates and customer satisfaction. By applying advanced regression techniques, we gained valuable insights that can guide proactive measures to enhance customer satisfaction.
